Address Points to APN

Last modified: 2020-05-12

This dataset comprises SITUS address points (as opposed to owner or mailing addresses) for jurisdictions within the County of San Diego.

Bike Master Plan

Last modified: 2020-05-12

Major infrastructure in the Bike Master Plan adopted in January 2014.

Bike route lines

Last modified: 2020-05-12

Existing (2015) Bike facilities in the San Diego Region. This dataset was developed for the primary purpose of updating the SANDAG San Diego Regional Bike Map and the interactive bike map on the iCommute website.

Business improvement districts

Last modified: 2020-05-12

A BID provides a business area with the resources to develop marketing campaigns, increase awareness and lobbying efforts, secure additional funding and enhance public improvement and beautification projects in partnership with the City.

City Council districts

Last modified: 2020-05-12

City of San Diego Council Districts Boundaries (adopted August 25, 2011).

Community Development Block Grant (CDBG) areas

Last modified: 2020-05-12

Census Tract Block Groups where at least 51 percent of residents are considered low and moderate income, according to the U.S. Housing and Urban Development’s Office of Community Planning and Development. CDBG funds are awarded for activities that benefit low- and moderate-income people.

Community planning districts

Last modified: 2020-05-12

Represents the geographic boundaries for the City of San Diego community land use policy plans specific to these geographic areas.

Fire battalion districts

Last modified: 2020-05-12

Fire Battalion districts have a chief that oversees 6 to 7 fire stations.

Fire Stations

Last modified: 2020-05-12

Data covering the location and status of incorporated and unincorporated Fire stations.

General Plan Land Use

Last modified: 2020-05-12

This dataset represents adopted planned land use polygons for City community planning areas.

Historical districts

Last modified: 2020-05-12

Historical districts contain multiple properties and/or objects that share historical significance. The City’s Historical Resources Board can establish a district if the contributing resources meet one of six criteria.

Transit Stops Locations

Last modified: 2020-05-12

Public transit stops and stations covering the County of San Diego.

Transit Routes Lines

Last modified: 2020-05-12

Public transit routes covering the County of San Diego.

Lifeguard station point locations

Last modified: 2020-05-12

Point locations for lifeguard stations on San Diego beaches. The City has nine permanent stations in Ocean Beach, Mission Beach, Pacific Beach, La Jolla, and Black’s Beach as well as stations operated seasonally.

Maintenance assessment districts

Last modified: 2020-05-12

A Maintenance Assessment District pays additional taxes to receive services beyond the general services the City provides to other areas. These services include landscape, lighting, and hardscape maintenance.

Multi-Habitat Planning Areas

Last modified: 2020-05-12

The Multi-Habitat Planning Area is planned habitat preserve. Of the 56,831 acres, 90 percent is targeted for preservation. Within this area, development is limited, but the public is allowed access in many areas for recreational activities.

Park Locations

Last modified: 2020-05-12

Properties managed by the City of San Diego Parks & Recreation Department.

Police Beats

Last modified: 2020-05-12

San Diego Police Department beats.

Police divisions

Last modified: 2020-05-12

The City has 9 divisions for the purpose of law enforcement: Central, Northern, Northeastern, Northwestern, Southern, Southeastern, Easter, Western, and Mid-City. Each division is a combination of law beat areas within the division boundary. These are police divisions only and do not represent legal neighborhood or community boundaries.

Police neighborhoods

Last modified: 2020-05-12

Law beat areas for the purpose of law enforcement within the City. These combine into larger police divisions. These boundaries are for law enforcement only and do not represent legal neighborhood or community boundaries.

Promise Zone boundary

Last modified: 2020-05-12

Promise Zone is a federal designation for an area of San Diego that has high unemployment, poverty, low educational attainment, insufficient access to healthcare and healthy foods, rising crime rates, and the least affordable housing in the country.

Recreation Center Locations

Last modified: 2020-05-12

Recreation centers operated by the City of San Diego Park and Recreation Department for active and passive recreational opportunities.

Revolving Loan Fund Coverage

Last modified: 2020-05-12

The San Diego Regional Revolving Loan Fund operates in San Diego and Chula Vista to provide small business assistance and generate neighborhood commercial district revitalization.

Streets Repair Line Segments

Last modified: 2020-05-12

Shapefile of the City’s street network. This shapefile contains the street segments identified in our streets repair projects and Overall Condition Index datasets. This data is displayed on streets.sandiego.gov{:target=”_blank” rel="noopener”}.

Roads Lines

Last modified: 2020-05-12

This dataset comprises centerline segments for roads (both active and inactive, public and private, constructed or of record) in San Diego County based on data received from all official jurisdictions within the County (the County and 18 cities).

San Diego city boundary

Last modified: 2020-05-12

Polygon representing the City of San Diego administrative boundary.

Zoning

Last modified: 2020-05-12

This dataset is a collection of the current base zone designations applied to property in the City of San Diego, as per the Official Zoning Map adopted by the City Council on February 28, 2006, and all subsequent updates.

Library Locations

Last modified: 2020-04-28

Point feature layer of City of San Diego library locations with associated website and contact information, created by the County of San Diego Department of Public Works GIS, in conjunction with San Diego County Library (SDCL).

Right-of-way parcels

Last modified: 2019-09-17

This is a subset of a countywide parcels dataset maintained by SanGIS. Right-of-way parcels are those that are legally dedicated to unhindered public travel. SanGIS uses county, state and federal sources to update this data.

Joint use parks

Last modified: 2019-09-17

Joint use facilities are intended to provide active and passive recreational opportunities for school children when school is in session and the general public when school is not in session.
